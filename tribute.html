Lieutanant Adnan

Malaysian hero, bravery, sacrifice, Bukit Chandu

Lieutenant Adnan Saidi: Defending Singapore Against Overwhelming Odd

Here's a time line of Lieutanant Adnan's life:

1920 - Adnan attends school in Sungei Ramal and showcases his academic abilities.

1933 - Adnan starts his teaching career but chooses to join the newly formed Malay Regiment, seeking new opportunities.

1934 - Adnan excels in military training, becoming the regiment's best recruit.

1936 - Adnan earns a promotion to sergeant, demonstrating leadership potential.

1937 - Adnan represents Malaya in the coronation parade of King George VI in London, receiving recognition for his service. The Second Sino-Japanese War (1937-1945): Erupts, raising concerns about Japanese expansionism in Asia.

1939 - Adnan starts a family, highlighting his personal life amid a world facing increasing tensions.

1940 - Japan occupies French Indochina (present-day Vietnam, Laos, and Cambodia), further expanding its influence in Southeast Asia. The Battle of Britain (1940): Takes place, highlighting the growing air war in Europe.

1941 - Adnan completes officer training and becomes a Lieutenant, leading a platoon in the Malay Regiment. Japan attacks Pearl Harbor and invades Malaya: Marks the start of the Pacific War (1941-1945) and the Malayan Campaign (1941-1942).

1942 - Lieutenant Adnan leads his platoon in the Battle of Pasir Panjang, fiercely defending Bukit Chandu against the invading Japanese forces. He is captured and executed on February 14th.
